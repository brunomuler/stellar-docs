How user-to-user cross-border payments work on Stellar

Overview

  • Laura in the USA wants to send money to Victor in Brazil.
  • Both Laura and Victor already have digital wallets they use to store, transfer, and trade Stellar-network tokens.
  • Laura wants to send her local currency, USD, but Victor wants to receive his local currency, BRL.
  • The following diagram shows how that payment – which involves 2 users, 2 wallets, and 2 anchors – unfolds on Stellar.
  • It assumes both wallets and both anchors support SEP-6 and SEP-12. The anchor to wallet interactions are all handled via API.

Here’s what happens in the background

  • 1. Laura Funds her account
    • Laura starts her deposit
    • Laura's KYC verification
    • Laura deposits USD and receives USD tokens
  • 2. Laura sends money to Victor
    • Laura starts transfer
    • USD-to-BRL conversion
    • Transfer is complete
  • Victor withdraws from his wallet
    • Victor starts his withdrawal
    • Victor's KYC Verification
    • Victor's withdrawal is completed
1

Laura funds her account

Laura starts her deposit

  • Laura opens her wallet app and enters the amount of USD she'd like to deposit.
  • The wallet pings AnchorUSA's Transfer Server with info about Laura, and about the deposit she'd like to make.
  • AnchorUSA's Transfer Server responds and requests whatever additional information it might need about Laura or her transfer. Since, in our example, AnchorUSA requires KYC info, it includes the required KYC fields in the response.
  • Laura opens her wallet app and enters the amount of USD she'd like to deposit.

Laura's KYC verification

  • Laura is prompted to upload the required KYC info to her wallet. If she uploaded it for a previous transaction, the wallet may already have that info on hand.
  • The wallet sends Laura’s KYC info to AnchorUSA’s /customer endpoint.
  • The anchor analyzes Laura’s KYC info -- how they do that is up to them -- and lets the wallet know if Laura passes. If she doesn’t, they let the wallet know why.
  • If Laura passes KYC, the wallet app lets her know she can continue her deposit. If she needs to provide additional info to pass KYC, the app gives her a chance to do that, too.

Laura deposits USD and receives USD tokens

  • Laura completes her USD deposit to AnchorUSA's account via ACH, wire, or whatever method AnchorUSD supports. That transfer happens on a traditional banking system, so it can take a few days.
  • AnchorUSA detects Laura’s deposit as it arrives and transfers USD tokens to her wallet’s Stellar account. That transfer happens on the Stellar network, so it just takes a few seconds.
2

Laura sends money to Victor

Laura starts transfer

  • Using her funded wallet, Laura specifies the amount of USD she’d like to send, and the currency Victor would like to receive, BRL.

USD-to-BRL conversion

  • The wallet executes a pathpayment on the Stellar network, which debits USD from Laura’s account, converts it to BRL using the built-in network orderbooks, and credits Victor with that BRL, all in a single transaction.

Transfer is complete

  • Victor sees the BRL in his wallet app
3

Victor withdraws from his wallet

Victor starts his withdrawal

  • Victor opens his wallet app and enters the amount of BRL he’d like to withdraw. He provides information about his Brazilian bank account, which is where he will receive the funds.
  • The wallet pings AnchorBrazil’s Transfer Server to pass along Victor’s withdrawal request and bank account info.
  • Like AnchorUSA, AnchorBrazil requires KYC info, so it responds with a request detailing the required KYC fields.
  • The wallet interface presents that info to Victor in a friendly way.

Victor's KYC Verification

  • Victor's KYC proceeds like Laura's, above

Victor's withdrawal is completed

  • When Victor passes AnchorBrazil’s KYC check, his wallet pings AnchorBrazil's Transfer Server again.
  • AnchorBrazil’s Transfer Server responds with AnchorBrazil’s Stellar address.
  • Victor’s wallet sends the specified amount of BRL tokens to AnchorBrazil’s Stellar address. This transaction happens on the Stellar network, and only takes a few seconds.
  • AnchorBrazil transfers an equivalent amount of BRL from their bank account to Victor’s bank account via ACH. That transfer happens on a traditional banking system, so it can take a few days.
